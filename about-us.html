Introduction to Womens Health Voice

Women’s health voice is a non-government, not for profit, charitable organization established in 2019 dedicated to work towards improving the health and wellbeing of women and children in the marginalized communities in Tanzania through better education, research and community outreach (home visits) to ensure the safety of both mother and child. Also we trains health professionals and community health workers deliver better care to mothers and young children which is culturally appropriate.

Our major problem

Despite of Tanzania being one of the relatively fast-growing countries in Africa, still has very high maternal mortality rate. Poor maternal, newborn, and child health indicators in Tanzania particularly in rural communities is attributed by the fact that many women prefer to deliver at home rather than at the nearest health facility. Neonatal deaths are inseparably linked to the health of the mother during pregnancy and to the conditions of delivery and newborn care. High rates of maternal, neonatal and child mortality continue to be a human tragedy in Tanzania. Many women dies from preventable pregnancy related complications. More than half of births in Tanzania occur at homes and this contributes to the elevated maternal mortality rate. Fewer than half of all deliveries are attended by a Nurse or Midwife. Majority of these deaths are ocuuring in the absence or lack of any medical help. This is the most distressing thing to us because almost all cases could have been avoided from happening. Pregnancy and childbirth complications are the leading cause of death among women and adolescent girls. The Women’s Health Voice is committed to work in poor rural communities to send information to unreached pregnant women in rural Tanzania.

Our belief

We believe that Health is a human right and maternal health requires empowerment and respect for the rights of women and girls as well as comprehensive health services.

Women`s Health voices history

The Women’s Health Voice (WHV) is a charitable Tanzania based organization which aims to demonstrably improve the health and well being of children in the remote communities in Tanzania through education, research and community outreach. WHV was created as a means of giving back to the community particularly the marginalized communities in Tanzania with health education and information. WHV aimed to address reproductive, maternal, newborn, child, and adolescent health challenges in the remote communities in Tanzania to impact and transform remote communities. Our mission accelerate achievements towards the UN Sustainable Development Goals. Join us as we strive to effect significant reduction in maternal and child mortality
